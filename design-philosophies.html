The Eight Principles

PRINCIPLE 01
Intentionality

Purpose

Design is making something with intention. Every feature asks for the user's time, attention, and trust — valuable things you can't afford to waste. Choosing what not to include is often more important than what to build.

  • Create value — every feature must earn its place
  • Stay focused on how people actually use the product
  • Find new ways to solve problems without re-creating existing solutions
PRINCIPLE 02
Empowerment

Agency

Put people in control. Let them do things their way instead of funneling them down a predetermined path. Offer forgiveness — make it easy to undo actions and confirm destructive operations. Agency gives people confidence to explore freely.

  • Stay out of the way — get users directly to their task
  • Give freedom to explore without locking into specific flows
  • Build forgiveness — make reversing actions effortless
PRINCIPLE 03
Trust

Responsibility

Act in people's best interest. Privacy is a human right — only request data when necessary and be transparent about its use. Anticipate misuse, especially with AI capabilities. Add safeguards and remove features if risks outweigh value.

  • Be transparent about what your product does from first interaction
  • Ask for permission in context, not at launch
  • Keep information safe — process data on-device when possible
PRINCIPLE 04
Intuition

Familiarity

Build on what people already know. Use metaphor that draws on real-world experience. Be consistent — things that look the same should behave the same. Consistent placement helps users predict where controls will be.

  • Standards create comfort — borrow familiar patterns
  • Balance with brand expression — know when to deviate
  • Provide clear feedback for every interaction
PRINCIPLE 05
Adaptability

Flexibility

People use your design in unique ways across different contexts. Support the full range of devices — touch on phone, pointer on laptop, hands-free in car. Cater to diverse abilities, age, language, and expertise.

  • Design for everyone — accessibility from the start
  • Consider varied input methods — voice, touch, keyboard, pointer
  • Preserve context across platforms with natural transitions
PRINCIPLE 06
Clarity

Simplicity

Strip away the unnecessary so the core purpose shines. Simplicity is not minimalism — burying all features in one place is not simple. Be concise with plain language and fewer steps. Establish clear hierarchy through order, spacing, and contrast.

  • Obsessive reduction — cut non-essential elements first
  • The three-tap rule — most actions within three taps or clicks
  • Reveal complexity only when absolutely necessary
PRINCIPLE 07
Execution

Craft

Attention to detail that tells people you care. Beautiful fonts across devices, thoughtful colors that adapt to light and dark, responsive animations with natural feedback, reliable foundations. Quality sets the tone — every element shows how much you care.

  • Be deliberate with every decision — fonts, colors, motion
  • Experiment and iterate — prototype early, discard what doesn't work
  • Maintain your craft — shipping isn't the finish line
PRINCIPLE 08
Joy

Delight

Not confetti or tacked-on flourishes. Delight is the natural result of getting all the other principles right. When you design with intent, focus, and care, the result is naturally delightful — it's the culmination of purpose, agency, familiarity, and craft working in harmony.

  • Identify the emotion you want to inspire
  • Create defining moments in every interaction
  • Don't mistake delight for decoration — serve the purpose first

Design is an ongoing commitment

A premium app is not defined by one big feature but by the sum of many small, intentional decisions — press feedback, motion, tactile response, and thoughtful empty states.
