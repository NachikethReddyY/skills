# Synthesis Format

Monthly synthesis transforms accumulated lessons into understanding. It's the meta-skill: learning about what you've learned. Run every 4-6 weeks.

## Template

```md
# Synthesis: {Month} {Year}

## What Did I Learn?
{New concepts, skills, and insights acquired this month. Link to each via `[[wiki-links]]`.}

## What Themes Kept Appearing?
{Patterns or ideas that showed up across multiple lessons or domains.}

## What Ideas Became More Important Than Expected?
{Concepts that seemed minor at first but turned out to be central.}

## What Assumptions Changed?
{Beliefs that were revised or abandoned this month.}

## What Still Doesn't Make Sense?
{Ongoing confusions and open questions. Link to CONFUSIONS.md and QUESTIONS.md.}

## Connections Discovered
{Unexpected links between domains. Add to CONNECTIONS.md.}

## Knowledge Compression
{Reduce this month's learning to: 1 page, 10 bullets, 3 principles, 1 sentence. See `references/SKILL.md` for compression format.}
```

## Rules

- **Run monthly, not ad-hoc.** Pick a day (e.g. last day of the month). Consistency matters more than exact timing.
- **Be honest about gaps.** "What still doesn't make sense" is the most valuable section for planning next month.
- **Update the knowledge map.** After synthesis, review the knowledge map and restructure if the hierarchy no longer fits.
- **Update patterns and connections.** Synthesis often reveals patterns that weren't visible week-to-week.
- **Keep it short.** One page max. If it runs longer, compress again.
